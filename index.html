The final tab, named "Data Biz Viz", is going to be a business case study I imagined. I guess it would be possible to build a city-attractivity KPI based on the price and number of travel published/seats booked every Friday on Blablacar. Why this study ? I have met a lot of people thanks to Blablacar, and it is clearly a melting pot. I think it gives a fair and accurate representation of French travellers today, and can therefore be one of the most effective way of knowing how attractive a city is with open-data. Why using the price ? It is a B2C platform, with drivers and passengers agreeing on a price it costs to go from a city to another (agreeing means driver publishing, passenger booking). [MAYBE PUT ALL THIS DIRECTLY ON THE DATA BIZ VIZ PAGE : Of course it is biased (what if some city has a direct train line to another, e.g Rennes-Brest, while another don't e.g Nantes-Brest, of course some trips will be biased. But I am positively sure that building such a KPI is possible. It won't be a KPI one can use to compare two cities, but more like a KPI that a city can watch to know its attractivity level. Like : prices drop, but the gas prices didn't go down. What is the problem ? Too many cars but not enough passengers : why ? Or more simply : oh passengers dropped 15% this week compared to 2 months ago. This loss was linear though time. No event can explain this drop (like some discount on trains, or people raising their fees), what is happening ? There is a chance this KPI is not relevant, but I cannot tell until I try).]

Now that you know almost everything about this website, let me introduce myself. I would start by saying that I am curious. I am always craving for new experiences. Therefore I guess I am not quite the same person everyday. I love running, soccer, rugby, acting, but I have been between places the past few years, and therefore I haven't been able to settle in a team recently. I started bouldering with my sister last summer, and I must say I quite enjoy the experience, because that's an activity that looks like it's easy and can be practiced solo, but it isn't. First, you have to analyze, plan your route. It is a kind of mind game. Second, you have to practice, develop some technical moves and identify your common failures. And this is easier and faster to do when there is someone watching you from the ground who can give you tips while you are climbing, and giving you advice after you climbed, to help you get better. And you also get better by watching others climbing.

Why am I looking for a job in data? In the year 2018, everybody was talking about data. It was said to provide great insights on someone's business, able to predict people's actions. I wanted to know what was so magical about data. So I took a few classes in Big Data. It wasn't magical, but our dataset were not "big", and our means really limited. So I applied for interships in data teams. Maybe there is enough data in businesses to make it magical. Still no magic, but a whole world of possibilities : data analyst, data engineer, data scientist, are just the tip of the iceberg. I am still curious ahout data, and I feel that there are still a lot of things that I don't know about it. And I am eager to learn. I assisted to a conference in 2018, from a man who worked for the GIGN, Bernard Thellier. At the end of his speech, he said : "One day, I woke up to go to work, and I told myself "Why ?".
